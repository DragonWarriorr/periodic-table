Keywords

The Age of Enlightenment

Period of rigorous philosophical, scientific and political advances, it is also called the Age of Reason.

It was also an intellectual and philosophical movement, it mainly happened in the 18th century.

Atomic Theory

Theory that matter consists of discrete units named atoms. (All atoms of an element have identical properties and mass, compounds are formed of a compound of 2 or more varieties of atoms.) An rearrangement of atoms is a chemical reaction.

According to Dalton’s atomic theory, different types of atoms can be distinguished by their atomic weight.

The Science Revolution

Period of drastic change in scientific thought, mainly occurred in the 16th and 17th centuries.

The Industrial Revolution

Transition from making things manually to using machines, happened around 1760 to 1840.

Spectrum Analysis

Calculations of oscillations/waves in a set of sequenced data. The spectrum that the name refers to is intensity vs wavelength or frequency and was first used in 1860.

Valence

Electrons involved in/available in a chemical bond formation.

Atomic weight

Total weight of an atom.

Philosopher's Stone

An object that can turn an ordinary metal into gold.

Nuclei

Positively charged central core of an atom, consisting of neutrons and protons, which contain almost all of its mass.

Neutron

Subatomic particle of around same mass as a proton, has no electric charge, is present in all atomic nuclei except hydrogen.

Electronegativity

Tendency of an atom to attract electrons towards itself, is affected by atomic nuclei and the distance that its valence electrons are from the charged nuclei.

Electron affinity

Energy change that was produced by adding an electron to a gaseous form.

Ionization energy

Quantity of energy that is isolated, gaseous atom in the ground electronic state must absorb to discharge an electron. This results in a cation.

Oxidation state

Hypothetical charge of an atom if all of its bonds to different atoms were fully ionic. It describes the degree of oxidation/loss of electrons of an atom in a chemical compound.

Quantum mechanics

Provides a description of physical properties in nature at the scale of subatomic particles and atoms. In quantum mechanics, momentum, energy, angular momentum and other qualities of a bound system are restricted to quantization(discrete values)

Old quantum theory

•Quantum mechanics arose because of theories to explain scientific observations that could not be explained by classical physics.

•For example, the correspondence between energy and frequency in Albert Einstein’s 1905 paper (that explained the photoelectric effect).

•Another example of this is Max Planck’s solution in 1900 to the black-body radiation problem.

This is what became known as old quantum theory, the early attempts to understand microscopic phenomena.

This is what led to the larger development of quantum mechanics in the mid 1920s.

The Manhattan Project

Development and research undertaking during World War Two, which produced the first ever nuclear weapons.

Analogous compounds

Elements in the same column within periodic table will have same/similar properties as each other.

When a molecule has 1 element replaced with another element from the same column, the 2 columns are called analogous.

Relativistic effects

Discrepancies between values calculated by models that consider relativity and those that do not.

Velocity

The speed of something in each direction.

Momentum

Quantity of motion of a moving body, measured as a product of velocity and mass.

Angular momentum

Quantity of rotation of a body, product of angular velocity and inertia.

Angular velocity

Rate of change of angular position of a rotating body.

Inertia

If a body is at rest, it will stay at rest until it is acted on by a force.

On the other hand, if a body is travelling at a constant speed in a straight line, it will continue moving like this unless it is acted on by a force.
