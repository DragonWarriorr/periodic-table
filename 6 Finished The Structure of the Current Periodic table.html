The Structure of the Current Periodic Table

Structure of the Current Periodic Table: Alkali metals

Alkali metas have 5 main properties:

•1.When exposed to air, they tarnish due to oxidation.

•2.They are very reactive.

•3.They are shiny, soft metals.

•4.they are soft enough to be cut with a knife.

•5.In the most external shell, they all have 1 valence electron. They usually try to lose this valence electron so they can have a full outer shell. This is what makes them so reactive.

The order of abundance of alkali metals, currently is:

•1.Sodium

•2.Potassium

•3.Lithium

•4.Rubidium

•5.Cesium

•6.Francium

When going down the periodic table, alkali metals have increasing relativity and increasing atomic radius.

Did you know that all current alkali metals have odd atomic numbers?

Structure of the Current Periodic Table: Alkaline-earth metals

"

The main properties of alkaline-earth metals are:

•They have 2 valence electrons.

•Low ionization energy.

•Low electron affinity.

•Low electronegativity.

•Highly reactive.

•Oxidation state of +2.

•Frequently form divalent cations. Examples of divalent cations are iron, calcium and magnesium.

Structure of the Current Periodic Table: Transition metals

The main properties of transition meals are:

•Large charge/radius ratio.

•Hard.

•High densities.

•Higher boiling and melting points than most metals.

•Form compounds, which are usually paramagnetic.

•Tend to show variable oxidation states.

•Can form compounds with profound catalytic activity.

•Can form coloured ions and compounds.

Notably, transition metals have valence electrons in their inner shell, meanwhile, most other types of elements have valence electrons in their outer shell.

Structure of the Current Periodic Table; Halogens, Group 17

Halogens are all highly reactive non-metals. They form singly charged anions (negative ions) and form similar compounds.

Halogens usually have extremely similar properties. However, there are differences when halogens are pure, for example:

•At room temperature, bromine is a liquid.

•At room temperature, chlorine and fluorine are gases.

•At room temperature, iodine is a solid.

Primarily, these differences are caused by increasing atomic masses as you go down the group.

To attain a filled shell of electrons, group 17 elements easily gain 1 electron. Due to this, all Group 17 elements frequently happen as anions (negative ions) in ionic compounds. This is commonly with positive ions of alkali metals. (E.g., sodium chloride (NaCl): consists of (1)positively charged sodium ion and (2)negatively charged chloride ions.)

•Did you know that the word 'halogen' derives from the Greek word 'hals', meaning salt? This is because the first 4 elements reacted with metals to produce compounds, which were similar to common salts.

Structure of the Current Periodic Table: Noble gases

•Noble gases are inert/unreactive gases. They can be made to form compounds.

•Noble gases are so inert because they have filled electron shells. Atoms of these elements are so inert that they do not even bond with their own kind. They are monatomic (consisting of 1 atom).

•They have very low boiling points. The boiling point relates to the atomic mass, so it increases down the table. For example, out of any element, helium has the lowest boiling point of -269 degrees Celsius. Meanwhile, radon (the heaviest naturally occurring noble gas, which is radioactive), boils at -61 degrees Celsius.

•The atoms of Group 18 elements are spherical because it is a condition that applies to atoms with half-filled or wholly filled orbitals.

Structure of the Current Periodic Table: Rare earth elements or Lanthanoid elements

In rare earths, the word ‘earths’ refers to metal oxides. It relates to a time when the existence of previously unknown metals can be inferred by identifying unfamiliar earths in minerals.

Characteristics of Lanthanoid elements include:

•Reactive.

•Can form ionic compounds with non-metals.

•They quickly lose their shine when exposed to air, ergo, they tarnish. To prevent tarnishing, they can be stored in noble gases or oil.

•Most lanthanoids only use 3 electrons as their valence electrons.

•They have atomic numbers, starting from 57 and ending with 70.

Structure of the Current Periodic Table: Actinoid elements

The nuclei in actinoids spontaneously disintegrate, releasing subatomic particles and transmuting into different elements.

Elements in the actinoid series with atomic numbers higher than uranium’s were discovered due to their being synthesized in nuclear reactors since the middle of the 20th century.

Actinides are referred to by the symbol ‘An’.

Actinides= metallic elements, which have atomic numbers starting from 89 and ending at 103.

The properties of actinoid elements include:

•Radioactive.

•Highly electropositive.

•Tarnish readily in air.

•Pyrophoric (ignite spontaneously in air).

•Very dense metals with distinctive structures.

•React with boiling water or dilute acid to release hydrogen gas.

•Tend to be fairly soft, with some being so soft that a knife can cut them.

•Paramagnetic.

•Solid at room pressure and temperature.

•Most are prone to hybridization.

•Combine directly with most non-metals.
